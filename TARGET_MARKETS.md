# Website Studio — Target Markets

## Ideal Client Profile

**The kind of business that has real opportunity online but hasn't captured it yet.**

They are:
- Established (2+ years in business) — not a startup gamble
- Legitimate and credible — real customers, real operations, real reputation
- Operating in a market where online presence affects customer acquisition
- Losing opportunities they don't fully measure — inquiries that don't convert, no-shows, reliance on word-of-mouth or a single platform (Google Maps, Facebook, etc.)
- Potentially losing to competitors with better web presence
- Not already well-served by a modern, high-quality website

They are NOT:
- A business that already has a strong, modern, conversion-optimised site
- A micro-business with no digital customer acquisition channel at all
- A large enterprise with a dedicated marketing team and budget
- A business in a highly regulated industry requiring complex procurement
- A business that has explicitly said they don't want a website

## Target Market Categories

### Primary — Local Professional Services
**Why:** High impact, low digital maturity, real customer acquisition pain, often run by owners who are brilliant at their craft but not web-savvy.

- Trades (plumbers, electricians, painters, roofers, carpenters, HVAC)
- Local contractors and construction firms
- Beauty and wellness (salons, clinics, aesthetics, holistic health)
- Professional services (accountants, solicitors, immigration consultants, financial advisors)
- Home services (cleaning, gardening, pest control, locksmiths)

**Why they work:** Their customers Google locally. Their website is often a afterthought from 2009. A modern, trust-building site with clear contact/call-to-action directly drives qualified inquiries.

**Avoid:** Businesses that are purely referral-based with no digital customer journey.

---

### Secondary — Clinics, Wellness, and Health
**Why:** Trust is everything. A polished, professional website is a trust signal before the patient even walks through the door.

- Dental clinics, physiotherapy, osteopathy, chiropractic
- Nutritionists, dietitians, mental health practitioners
- Yoga studios, Pilates, specialist fitness
- Aesthetic clinics, skincare, dermatology

**Why they work:** Patients research providers online. A professional, calming, informative site converts browsers to bookings. Many are running on outdated бро bearing or Facebook pages.

**Avoid:** Large hospital groups or chain clinics with existing marketing teams.

---

### Tertiary — Boutique Hospitality and Food
**Why:** Instagram has replaced the website for many, but a restaurant/cafe with genuine character still benefits enormously from a proper site — especially for events, reservations, and brand differentiation.

- Independent restaurants, cafes, gastropubs
- Food brands with a direct-to-consumer angle (not just retail)
- Bakeries, patisseries, specialty food producers
- Boutique hotels, B&Bs, event venues

**Why they work:** Visual identity matters enormously. A site that matches the quality of the product creates brand coherence and drives reservations/orders.

**Avoid:** Chains with existing marketing departments. Fast food. Businesses purely relying on delivery platforms.

---

### Quaternary — Consultants, Coaches, and Specialists
**Why:** Personal credibility is the product. A website that looks amateur undermines an otherwise strong personal brand.

- Business coaches, life coaches, career consultants
- Specialist recruiters, headhunters
- Marketing consultants, PR specialists
- Training providers, tutors, educators
- Legal consultants, compliance advisors

**Why they work:** For many consultants, the website IS the first impression of their expertise. A polished, clear site with evidence, testimonials, and a clear service offering converts cold traffic that would otherwise never reach out.

**Avoid:** Large consulting firms. Enterprise-level procurement cycles.

---

## Geographic Focus

**Primary: Ireland (Dublin + major cities initially)**
- Familiar market context — easy to verify, visit, understand
- Direct timezone for potential future delivery
- Strong SMB ecosystem with many businesses underinvested in digital
- Geographic constraint helps focus the research pipeline

**Secondary: UK (near-future)**
- Same language, similar market dynamics
- Same timezone (GMT)
- Large addressable market

**Why not global initially:** Quality research at depth requires local context knowledge. Staying geographically focused allows Scout to produce much better quality research with less friction.

## First Test Segment

**Decision: Dublin trades + professional services (Tier 1)**

Rationale:
- Gbemi already has outreach infrastructure for this segment (outreach system built in March 2026)
- Existing prospect database from the Verdantia cold outreach work
- High volume of clear opportunity gaps
- Strong fit for the Tier 2 homepage concept approach
- Can repurpose and extend existing mockup/asset infrastructure

**First campaign scope:** 20–30 leads from Dublin-based trades and professional services, with zero external outreach until 5 leads are fully qualified, researched, and approved by Nero.
