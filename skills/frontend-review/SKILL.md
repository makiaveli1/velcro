# Skill: Frontend Design Review Rubric

Use when asked to review a frontend design, UI screenshot, landing page, or component layout.

## Review Rubric

### 1. Visual Hierarchy
- Is the most important element immediately obvious?
- Are headings, subheadings, and body text clearly differentiated?
- Is whitespace used purposefully to guide the eye?

### 2. Color & Contrast
- Are all text-to-background contrast ratios at least 4.5:1 (WCAG AA)?
- Is color used purposefully, not decoratively?
- Is there a consistent color palette?

### 3. Typography
- Are fonts consistent in weight, size, and family?
- Is text legible at common viewport sizes?
- Is line height adequate for readability?

### 4. Layout & Spacing
- Is the layout responsive?
- Are grid systems or spacing scales consistent?
- Is content appropriately sized for its importance?

### 5. Component Quality
- Are buttons, inputs, and cards clearly recognizable?
- Are interactive states (hover, focus, active) visible?
- Are form elements labeled correctly?

### 6. Visual Polish
- Are icons consistent in style and size?
- Is there visual balance across the page?
- Does it look intentional rather than default?

### 7.UX Patterns
- Are common patterns (nav, forms, cards) used correctly?
- Is the user flow clear?
- Are error states and empty states handled?

## Output Format
Give a structured review: Strengths, Issues Found (with severity), Recommendations.
Do not be vague. Reference specific elements.
