# Skill: Website Audit Rubric

Use when asked to audit a website for business, UX, or technical quality.

## Audit Categories

### 1. Business Clarity
- Is the value proposition immediately clear?
- Is there a clear CTA above the fold?
- Is the target audience addressed?
- Does the messaging match the product?

### 2. Trust Signals
- Are there social proofs (testimonials, logos, reviews)?
- Is contact info or company info present?
- Are privacy/security signals visible if relevant?

### 3. Navigation & Structure
- Is the information architecture clear?
- Can a user find what they need in 3 clicks or less?
- Is the navigation consistent?

### 4. Copy & Content
- Is the headline compelling and specific?
- Is body copy scannable (short paragraphs, bullets)?
- Does the content address objections?
- Is there a clear next step?

### 5. Technical Quality
- Page loads under 3 seconds on mobile?
- No broken links or 404s?
- Mobile-responsive?
- SEO basics in place (title, meta, semantic HTML)?

### 6. Conversion Design
- Are forms minimal and easy to complete?
- Is there friction reduction at every step?
- Are trust signals placed near CTAs?

## Output Format
Summary score per category (Strong/Weak/Problematic) + top 3 actionable findings.
Be specific about what loses the audience and what converts.
