# Skill: Outreach & Pitch Rubric

Use when reviewing outreach messages, cold emails, sales pitches, or commercial proposals.

## Review Checklist

### 1. Opening Hook
- Does it immediately show relevance to the recipient?
- Is there a reason to keep reading in the first sentence?
- Does it avoid generic opener ("I hope you're well", "I wanted to reach out")?

### 2. Problem Framing
- Is the problem stated in the recipient's terms, not the sender's?
- Is the stakes clear (so what?)?
- Is it specific, not vague?

### 3. Credibility Signal
- Is there a brief proof of competence or fit?
- Does it avoid hollow claims ("we're passionate about X")?
- Is there a concrete example or result?

### 4. Call to Action
- Is the ask clear and single?
- Is it easy to say yes to?
- Is there no ambiguity about what happens next?

### 5. Tone & Length
- Is the tone professional but human?
- Is it as short as possible without losing essential information?
- Does it avoid buzzwords and filler?

### 6. Personalization
- Is it clearly written for this specific recipient?
- Does it reference something specific about them (not just their company name)?
- Does it avoid obvious copy-paste errors?

## Output Format
Per-section rating: Strong / Weak / Missing + specific rewrite suggestions.
Keep feedback actionable. Rewrite the hook if the hook is weak.
