# Website Studio — Channel Policy

_For Round 1 and beyond. Defines which channels are used, when, and when not to use them._

---

## The One Rule

**Channel is a two-gate decision:**
1. Content and approach are approved by Nero (during normal approval queue)
2. Deployment (the specific act of sending) is separately approved by Nero — for each touch, each channel

This means even a fully approved pitch requires a separate explicit go-ahead from Nero before Mercury actually sends it.

---

## Round 1 Channel Decision

**For Dublin Trades Round 1: Email only.**

**Rationale:**
- Trades businesses (plumbers, electricians) are consumer-facing, not B2B — LinkedIn is not the natural discovery channel
- Email is the standard professional contact channel for small businesses
- We have a working Gmail API infrastructure from the Verdantia outreach work
- Email is documentable, reviewable, and reversible
- Web contact forms are low-value for cold outreach and require manual submission
- Starting with one channel reduces risk and simplifies learning

**LinkedIn is not used in Round 1.** LinkedIn may be considered in future rounds for B2B professional services (consultants, coaches, agencies) where LinkedIn is the natural professional discovery platform.

## What Counts as a Business Contact Point

### ✅ Acceptable contact points (Round 1)
- Business email address on their website (e.g., info@[domain].ie, contact@[domain].ie)
- Business email found in a directory listing (TrueROI, Golden Pages) where it is publicly listed
- Google Business Profile email field (if publicly visible)
- Web contact form on their existing website (public, not a gated inquiry form)

### ⚠️ Borderline contact points (require Nero approval before use)
- Personal email addresses found online (e.g., firstname.lastname@gmail.com)
- Social media DM handles for business accounts
- Any contact point that is not clearly a public business channel
- Generic "info@" addresses that appear to route to a larger organisation

### ❌ Never use as contact points
- Personal social media accounts (private profiles)
- Personal phone numbers (unless published as a business line)
- WhatsApp or messaging apps
- Any channel where the business has explicitly requested not to be contacted
- Leaked or scraped email lists
- Purchased or rented email lists of any kind

## Email Standards (Round 1)

### Finding the right address
**Preferred (in priority order):**
1. `info@[business-domain].ie` or similar — highest legitimacy
2. `contact@[business-domain].ie`
3. A specific person's email found on their website
4. A directory-listed email — acceptable if publicly listed

**Acceptable if no better option found:**
- A generic Gmail/Outlook address used as a business email — flag to Nero for approval

**Never use:**
- Emails inferred from guess patterns
- Emails found via Hunter.io without verification
- Emails for individuals at their workplace if not publicly listed

## When to Use Contact Forms Instead of Email

Use a web contact form **only if:**
- No email address can be found despite thorough search
- The contact form is prominently placed on their website (indicating they monitor it)
- The business is in a category where contact forms are the primary professional inquiry channel

**Do not use contact forms if:**
- The form appears to be for a CRM or sales inquiry system
- The form is buried, has not been updated, or appears unmonitored

**If in doubt:** Flag to Nero. Use email over contact form when uncertain.

---

## When to Skip a Lead Entirely

### Automatic skip (do not queue)
- No verifiable business contact point found
- Business has a prominent "do not contact" notice
- Business appears to be in active crisis (legal, financial, public scandal)
- Business is already heavily targeted by web design outreach
- The contact information is exclusively personal channels

### Escalate to Nero before proceeding
- Personal email address is the only contact point available
- Business has explicitly mentioned they are not looking for website services
- Business has an existing exclusive relationship with another web provider
- The business is in a sensitive category (medical, legal, financial)

## Opt-Out and Suppression Expectations

### If a business replies "not interested"
- Suppress immediately in CRM/SUPPRESSION.md
- Log in lead STATUS.md
- Do not follow up regardless of sequence timing
- Reply graciously once: "Totally understand. No problem at all. Best of luck with the business."

### If a business replies "remove me" / "do not contact"
- Suppress immediately
- Reply once: "Understood — apologies for reaching out, you have been removed from our list."
- No further contact under any circumstances

### If a business is already receiving high-volume outreach
**Signs:** Multiple cold email timestamps in site source, multiple "web design" Google Ads for their category

**Action:** Skip them. Do not add to the pile.

## Channel Discipline for Round 1

1. **Email is the only approved outbound channel for Round 1.**
2. **LinkedIn is not used in Round 1.**
3. **Contact forms are a fallback only — Nero approval required before use.**
4. **Every send requires a separate explicit Nero deployment approval.**
5. **Suppression is checked manually before Mercury executes any send.**
