# Website Studio — Lead Scorecard

## Purpose

Scout uses this scorecard to evaluate every potential lead. The score determines what action to take. Nothing advances without passing the qualification threshold.

**Golden rule:** A low-quality lead pitched is worse than no lead. Quality over quantity. Always.

---

## Scoring Framework

Each signal is scored. Add them up. The total determines the lead's disposition.

### Section A — Website Presence (0 to 30 points)

| Signal | Points | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| No website found at all | +15 | They have zero digital home. Highest opportunity. |
| Website exists but clearly dormant/inactive | +10 | Domain still live but no recent updates, no SEO activity |
| Social media only (Facebook/Instagram page as "site") | +8 | Effectively no proper web presence |
| Website found but clearly outdated (2014 or earlier aesthetic) | +10 | Visual age is obvious and measurable |
| Basic/static site, no mobile optimization | +8 | Mobile experience is broken or barely functional |
| Competitor has significantly better website | +5 | Context gap, not just their own site quality |

### Section B — Site Quality & Trust (0 to 30 points)

| Signal | Points | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| No trust signals (no address, no phone, no about page) | +8 | Cannot establish credibility |
| No customer testimonials or social proof anywhere | +5 | Trust gap is a major conversion blocker |
| Poor visual design that undermines the business quality | +8 | The site looks worse than the business actually is |
| Weak or generic copy (sounds like every competitor) | +4 | Messaging doesn't differentiate |
| No clear contact or call-to-action path | +6 | Visitor has no obvious next step |
| No HTTPS or visible security indicators | +4 | Particularly relevant for wellness/medical |
| Logo looks homemade/pixelated or obviously templated | +3 | Low-cost signal that doesn't match "real business" |

### Section C — Business Legitimacy Signals (0 to 20 points)

| Signal | Points | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Clear business address (not just "Dublin") | +4 | Real physical presence |
| Google Business Profile with real reviews | +4 | Active and cared about online reputation |
| Multiple years in business (3+) | +4 | Established, not a fly-by-night |
| Active social media with engagement | +3 | Proof of active operations |
| Listed in relevant trade directories | +3 | Industry legitimacy signal |
| Business phone number matches domain | +2 | Consistency across channels |

### Section D — Opportunity Multipliers (0 to 20 points)

| Signal | Points | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Clear service offering with pricing on current site | +5 | They understand what they sell — easier concept |
| Market segment where website = credibility (wellness, medical, legal) | +5 | Website has outsized importance in this category |
| Appears to be growing (hiring, new locations, positive reviews trend) | +4 | More likely to invest in growth infrastructure |
| Customer journey involves research/consideration (not impulse) | +4 | People compare before choosing — website matters here |
| Business has a genuine story/USP worth telling | +2 | Rich concept material |

---

## Scoring Thresholds

| Score | Disposition | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 0–25 | ❌ IGNORE | Do not pursue. May be legitimate but opportunity is insufficient or too difficult to demonstrate. Log in suppression list. |
| 26–40 | 👁️ MONITOR | Not ready to pitch. Flag for periodic re-evaluation. If score improves significantly, revisit. |
| 41–55 | 🔍 AUDIT-WORTHY | Good candidate. Scout conducts full audit or no-site brief. Mercury produces concept direction. If concept is strong, escalate to pitch-worthy. |
| 56–70 | ✅ PITCH-WORTHY | Strong opportunity. Full research, concept brief, and pitch draft produced. Escalate to approval queue. |
| 71+ | 🌟 HIGH PRIORITY | Exceptional opportunity. Fast-track to approval queue. Nero reviews within 24 hours. |

---

## Do-Not-Pitch Rules

**Absolute disqualifiers — never pitch regardless of score:**

1. **No evidence of legitimate business operations** — no address, no reviews, no social media, no clear offering, all found via web search
2. **Active legal or regulatory issues** — businesses under investigation, serious unresolved complaints, or known fraud
3. **Explicit "do not contact" signals** — unsubscribe lists, explicit requests not to be contacted
4. **High-risk reputation situations** — businesses in active PR crises, public controversies
5. **Competitors of existing clients** — suppress to protect current relationships
6. **Businesses already being pitched by the same system** — no duplicates, ever
7. **Businesses with no clear digital customer journey** — if they genuinely operate 100% by referral and have no need for online acquisition
8. **Under 18 months in operation** — too much churn risk

---

## Scoring Process

1. **Lead identified** → Scout runs initial signal scan (5–10 minutes)
2. **Initial score calculated** → If 41+, proceed to full research
3. **Full research completed** → Score may be updated based on deeper findings
4. **Concept direction drafted** → If concept is weak or generic, downgrade or abandon regardless of score
5. **Final disposition** → Pitch-worthy leads enter approval queue

**The scorecard is a guide, not a prison.** If Scout finds compelling qualitative evidence that outweighs a low score, it can recommend escalation. Nero decides.

---

## Anti-Gaming Rules

- Do not inflate scores to justify pitching leads that don't deserve it
- Do not pitch businesses just because they're easy to research
- Do not pitch businesses in categories that are oversaturated with solutions
- Do not pitch businesses that have already been recently contacted by other services
- Quantity of leads is not a metric of success — quality of pitches is

**A single well-researched, high-quality lead with a specific concept is worth more than 50 spray-and-pray inquiries.**
