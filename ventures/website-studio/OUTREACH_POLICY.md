# Website Studio — Outreach Policy

## Core Rule

**No outbound communication of any kind is sent without explicit written approval from Nero.**

**Email cannot be sent until `studio@verdantia.it` passes the full Mailbox Readiness Checklist in `MAILBOX_SETUP.md`.** Outreach is hard-blocked until that checklist is fully satisfied.

**Mercury drafts. Mercury prepares. Mercury does NOT send anything without explicit, separate Nero approval to deploy.**

This applies to:
- Cold emails
- LinkedIn messages or connection requests
- DM-style outreach on any platform
- Follow-up messages
- Any communication that identifies the business, the sender, or the service

Approval is required for:
- Initial pitch content
- Follow-up message content
- Timing of send
- Channel selection
- Any variation or adaptation of approved content

---

## Permitted Activities (Internal Only)

These do NOT require approval because they are internal research and preparation:

- Web searches to find businesses
- Website auditing (reading public pages only)
- Business research via public sources (Google Maps, review sites, social media public posts, directories)
- Competitor analysis via public sources
- Concept brief creation (internal document)
- Pitch draft creation (internal document, clearly marked DRAFT)
- CRM updates and lead management
- Scorecard completion
- Internal review and discussion

## Suppression List

Businesses on the suppression list are never contacted, regardless of score or quality.

### Automatic Suppression (always suppressed)
- Any business that has explicitly asked not to be contacted
- Any business on the global do-not-contact registry
- Any business currently being pitched by another verified service
- Competitors of existing clients (to protect relationships)

### Nero-Declared Suppression
- Businesses Nero marks as suppression after review
- Businesses with reputation risk
- Businesses in categories that conflict with system values

**Suppression list location:** `CRM/SUPPRESSION.md`

## Message Standards

### What good outreach looks like:
- Specific: references something real about their business
- Respectful: doesn't insult their current website or business
- Evidence-based: references observations, not assumptions
- Low-pressure: no fake urgency, no aggressive sales tactics
- Clear CTA: one easy next step, no pressure
- Honest: doesn't misrepresent who we are or what we do

### What we NEVER do:
- **Never** imply we have already worked with them or know them
- **Never** say "I saw your website and was impressed" as a generic opener
- **Never** use clickbait subject lines
- **Never** send without the recipient's business name correct
- **Never** pitch multiple services at once
- **Never** use "just checking in" or "did you get my last email?" style follow-ups without genuine new context
- **Never** lie about why we're contacting them
- **Never** claim to be a "web design agency" sending bulk cold email
- **Never** send attachments unless explicitly relevant and approved

### Tone guidance:
- Write like a smart, direct human — not a sales template
- Treat them as a business peer, not a prospect to close
- Lead with insight, not with a sales ask
- The concept preview IS the value — lead with what we found, not what we want

## Follow-Up Rules

### Timing:
- Follow-up 1: Day 4 after initial send (if no response)
- Follow-up 2: Day 10 after initial send (if no response)
- Follow-up 3: Day 21 after initial send (if no response)
- Maximum: 3 total messages (1 initial + 2 follow-ups)

### Content:
- Each follow-up must have genuine new context or value — not just "bumping this"
- Acceptable new context: a related observation, a relevant market insight, a different angle on the concept
- Follow-up content MUST be approved by Nero before sending
- **Separate from content approval:** Nero must also separately approve the deployment of each follow-up touch — Mercury does not send until Nero explicitly says "go ahead" for that specific touch

### After 3 touches:
- Mark as NO_RESPONSE
- Add to suppression list for 6 months minimum
- Do not re-contact unless significant new information emerges

### Response handling:
- If they reply anything — even "not interested" — that counts as engagement. Log the response.
- If "not interested": respond graciously, thank them, close the lead gracefully
- If "not interested, remove me": immediately suppress and comply

---

## Volume Limits

**To maintain quality and prevent this from becoming spam:**

- Maximum 5 outreach sends per business day
- Maximum 20 new leads added to the active pipeline per week
- Maximum 50 leads in active research/pitch queue at any time
- If queue is full: pause discovery, process existing leads

**Rationale:** Each lead requires deep research and a custom approach. We cannot maintain quality at scale. Better to do 5 excellent pitches than 50 mediocre ones.

## Compliance and Risk

### What we protect:
- The reputation of the system and Likwid
- The legitimacy of every business we contact
- The quality standard of our outreach
- The businesses we contact from receiving genuine spam

### Red flags that halt outreach immediately:
- Business asks not to be contacted → suppress immediately
- Business mentions they have an agency or existing web provider → be respectful, do not disparage
- Business is in a sensitive category (health, legal, finance) → proceed with extra care and no claims of expertise we don't have
- Any sign the business is already overwhelmed with cold outreach → do not add to the pile

## What Happens After Outreach

- All responses are routed back through Nero
- Any "not interested" or opt-out is immediately suppressed and logged
- Any positive response triggers a Nero review before next action
- Outreach outcomes are tracked in `STATUS.md` and logged in the CRM
