# MONETIZATION_EXPERIMENTS.md — Monetization Experiments Runbook

_When proposing or evaluating monetization experiments, do this._

---

## What Is a Monetization Experiment?

A test of a specific way to generate revenue — pricing change, new offer, new pricing model, new channel, new tier. The goal is to learn fast and cheaply before committing.

---

## Step 1 — Define the Hypothesis

What are we testing?
- What change are we making?
- What do we expect to happen?
- Why do we think this will work?

State it clearly: "If we [change], then [expected outcome] because [reason]."

---

## Step 2 — Assess the Risk

What is the downside if this fails?
- Customer confusion or churn?
- Brand damage?
- Revenue loss?

What is the upside if it works?
- New revenue stream?
- Higher conversion?
- Better retention?

---

## Step 3 — Design the Experiment

**What exactly are we changing?**
- The offer?
- The price?
- The channel?
- The model?

**How will we measure success?**
- Revenue?
- Conversion rate?
- Customer acquisition cost?
- Retention?

**How long should it run?**
- Minimum 2 weeks for meaningful data
- Note what sample size is needed for statistical validity

**What's the rollback plan?**
- If it fails, how do we undo it?

---

## Step 4 — Resources Needed

- What does this cost to run?
- Time investment?
- Platform or tool requirements?

---

## Step 5 — Deliverable Format

```
## Monetization Experiment: [name]

### Hypothesis
"If we [change], then [expected outcome] because [reason]."

### What Is Changing
[specific change]

### Success Metrics
- Primary: [metric]
- Secondary: [metric]

### Target
[conversion rate / revenue target / etc.]

### Experiment Duration
[X weeks — minimum viable time for signal]

### Rollback Plan
[how to undo if it fails]

### Resources Needed
[cost, time, tools]

### Risks
- [risk 1] — [mitigation]
- [risk 2] — [mitigation]

### Confidence
HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW — [reason]

### Recommendation
[proceed / not proceed / modify before testing]

[DRAFT — NOT FOR DISTRIBUTION — REQUIRES NERO APPROVAL]
```

---

## Quality Rules

- Every experiment needs a clear hypothesis, not just a feeling
- Minimum viable sample size matters — small tests can be misleading
- Note what "success" actually means before running the experiment
- If we don't have a way to measure it, we don't run the experiment
