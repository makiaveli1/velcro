# LINKEDIN_DRAFTING.md — LinkedIn Post Drafting Runbook

_When drafting a LinkedIn post, do this._

---

## Step 1 — Understand the Goal

What is the post trying to accomplish?
- Build authority on a topic?
- Attract a specific audience?
- Drive engagement?
- Soft-sell a service?

Who is the audience?

---

## Step 2 — Choose the Post Type

| Type | Use when |
|---|---|
| Insight post | Positioning as an expert on a topic |
| Story post | Building personal connection |
| Proof post | Showing social proof or results |
| Contrarian post | Challenging a popular belief |
| Educational post | Providing value, building trust |
| CTA post | Driving to an offer or next step |

---

## Step 3 — Write the Draft

### Opening Hook (1-2 lines)
Something that stops the scroll. A bold claim, a question, a surprising stat.

### Body
- First paragraph: the hook and context
- Supporting points: 2-4 bullets or short paragraphs
- Keep it scannable

### Closer
- Tie back to the hook
- End with a question or CTA
- Keep it simple

---

## Step 4 — Format Rules

- Short paragraphs — 1-2 lines max
- White space — don't dump text
- No more than 3-4 hashtags at the end
- Avoid ALL CAPS
- Emojis: 1-2 max, only if they add meaning

---

## Step 5 — Deliverable Format

```

[what the hook is]

### CTA
[what the ask/closer is]

### Tags / Timing
[recommended hashtags, best time to post]

### Risks / Notes
[anything that might be controversial or that needs review]

### Confidence
HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW — [reason]

[DRAFT — NOT FOR DISTRIBUTION — REQUIRES NERO APPROVAL]
```

---

## Quality Rules

- Never draft something I wouldn't say in person
- Don't overclaim results without evidence
- Don't be vague — "transform your business" is not a LinkedIn post
- If the post touches a sensitive topic, flag it for Nero review
- No political, religious, or controversial content unless directly relevant to business
