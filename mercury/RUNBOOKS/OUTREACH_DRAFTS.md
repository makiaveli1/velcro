# OUTREACH_DRAFTS.md — Outreach Drafting Runbook

_When drafting cold outreach, do this._

---

## Cold Outreach Principles

Cold outreach is a permission game. The first message earns the right to the second. If the first message is about you, it fails.

Good cold outreach:
- Shows I did research on them specifically
- States a clear reason why I reached out
- Offers value before asking for time
- Has a specific, low-friction next step

Bad cold outreach:
- Generic "I help X companies with Y"
- Asks for a call immediately
- Oversells before establishing rapport
- No clear reason why I chose them specifically

---

## Step 1 — Research the Prospect

Before drafting, I need to know:
- Their role and what they care about
- Their company and what they're building or struggling with
- Why they might need what we're offering
- Any personal connection or common ground

If I don't have enough research, I ask Scout for it.

---

## Step 2 — Email Sequence Structure

### Email 1 — The Cold Introduction
**Subject line:** Specific + personal + low-pressure

**Body structure:**
1. Hook — reference something specific about them
2. Reason — why I reached out specifically to them
3. Value — what they get, not what I sell
4. CTA — one low-friction next step

Keep it under 100 words.

### Email 2 — Follow-up (3-5 days later)
Reference Email 1. No guilt. Add new value or perspective.

### Email 3 — Breakup (7-10 days after)
Last attempt. Confirm they saw it. Close with no pressure.

---

## Step 3 — LinkedIn DM Structure

Shorter than email. More direct.
1. Reference — something specific about their content or profile
2. Reason — brief, personal
3. Ask — specific, low-friction

Max 150 characters.

## Quality Rules

- Every message must reference something specific — not generic
- Never claim "I know exactly what you need" without evidence
- No fake urgency or manipulative tactics
- If I'm unsure about a prospect, I don't draft — I ask for more research
- Always include a real unsubscribe or stop option
