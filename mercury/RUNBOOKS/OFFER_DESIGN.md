# OFFER_DESIGN.md — Offer Design Runbook

_When designing or evaluating an offer, do this._

---

## What Is an Offer?

An offer is what we're selling — product, service, or package — and why someone would buy it. Not the product description. The transformation and the reason to buy now.

---

## Step 1 — Understand the Problem We're Solving

What is the core problem?
Who has this problem, specifically?
How are they solving it today (status quo)?

---

## Step 2 — Define the Offer Components

**The result** — what does the buyer get, specifically?
**The transformation** — what changes for them after?
**The evidence** — why should they believe this works?
**The timeframe** — when do they see results?

---

## Step 3 — Frame the Offer

Use this structure:

**For [specific audience]**
**Who are frustrated by [specific problem]**
**Our [product/service] provides [specific solution]**
**Unlike [competitor or status quo], we [key differentiator]**
**You get [specific outcomes] by [timeframe/conditions]**

---

## Step 4 — Pricing

- What does the market pay for similar?
- What value does the buyer get vs. what they pay?
- Pricing model: one-time, subscription, retainer, performance?
- What's the entry point vs. premium tier?

---

## Step 5 — Risk Reversal

What makes this easy to say yes to?
- Guarantee?
- Results or money back?
- Trial period?
- Low-friction start?

---

## Step 6 — Deliverable Format

```
## Offer Brief: [name]

### Problem
[Specific problem, specific audience]

### Status Quo
[How they're solving it today]

### Our Offer
[What they get — specific]

### Transformation
[What changes for them — specific, measurable where possible]

### Key Differentiator
[Why us — one clear reason]

### Evidence
[Proof points, testimonials, data]

### Pricing
[model + entry price]

### Risk Reversal
[What reduces buying friction]

### Ideal Client Profile
[Who this is specifically for]

### Next Step
[Clear CTA]

### Confidence
HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW — [reason]
[DRAFT — NOT FOR DISTRIBUTION — REQUIRES NERO APPROVAL]
```

---

## Quality Rules

- Specific beats vague — no "save time", use "save 3 hours per week"
- Don't claim results that can't be supported
- If pricing is TBD, note it and provide a range based on market
- Never finalize an offer without knowing who it's for
