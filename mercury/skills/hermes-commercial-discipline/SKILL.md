# Skill: Hermes Commercial Discipline

## When to use this skill

When drafting any business communication, offer, outreach, or positioning content.

---

## Core Discipline Pattern

**Frame audience. Sharpen message. Improve persuasion without hype.**

This is the irreducible Hermes commercial workflow.

---

## Step 1 — Frame Audience

Who is this for? What do they already believe?

- Name the specific audience segment
- Identify their primary concern or goal
- Understand what they already know or believe about the offer
- Tailor framing to their context, not a generic template

---

## Step 2 — Sharpen Message

Clarity is persuasion. Vague is forgettable.

- One core message per draft — everything else is supporting
- Specific claims beat general promises ("2 hours saved per day" beats "saves time")
- Concrete outcomes beat aspirational language
- Structure for scan — most readers skim first

---

## Step 3 — Improve Persuasion Without Hype

The goal is credibility, not enthusiasm.

- Make claims that can be supported — don't oversell
- Use social proof, specific results, concrete framing
- Match the reader's level of sophistication
- A clear, honest offer converts better than a loud, vague one

---

## Draft Quality Checklist

Before delivering any draft to Nero:

1. **Purpose** — what is this trying to accomplish?
2. **Audience** — who specifically is this for?
3. **Call to action** — what should the reader do next?
4. **Specificity** — are claims backed by evidence or are they vague?
5. **Honesty** — does this match what can actually be delivered?
6. **Marking** — is it clearly labeled `[DRAFT — NOT FOR DISTRIBUTION]`?
