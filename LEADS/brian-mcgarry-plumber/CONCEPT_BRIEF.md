# Brian McGarry Plumber — Concept Brief

## Business Essence

**What:** Sole-trader plumber serving Dublin 12, specifically Walkinstown and surrounding areas (Knocklyon, Crumlin, Drimnagh, Terenure).
**Who:** Dublin 12 homeowners — typically owner-occupiers, aged 35–65, with property maintenance needs ranging from emergency leaks to bathroom renovations. They search on mobile, evaluate trust quickly, and prefer to call someone local they feel they can hold accountable.
**Since:** 2014 — 11+ years in business. This is the single most important trust asset he has.
**The ONE thing:** A local man, with a local address, who has been fixing Dublin 12 homes for over a decade — and can be reached directly on his mobile.

**The business goal for this website:** Get found in local search + convert mobile browsers to phone calls. Every other consideration is secondary.

---

## The Core Website Problem

> When a Dublin 12 homeowner searches "plumber near me" or "plumber Dublin 12" on Google, they see a map pack of 3 competitors and a page of organic results. Brian McGarry does not appear in any of them. He has no website and no Google Business Profile. He has been invisible in digital local search since 2014, while his competitors — DeWAR Plumbers, Dublin Area Plumbers, NRM Plumbing — have accumulated reviews, map presence, and website traffic. Every month, Dublin 12 homeowners who would have chosen Brian McGarry cannot find him. This website is not a marketing exercise. It is a visibility intervention.

## Design Language

### Colour Palette

**Primary — Deep Charcoal Slate:** `#2D3436`
Not generic corporate blue. A grounded, serious slate that signals experience and competence without being cold. It reads "this person knows what they're doing" — the way a well-worn, quality tool looks.

**Background — Warm Off-White:** `#F8F5F0`
Not clinical white. A warm, slightly cream off-white that feels human and domestic. This is someone's home we're talking about — the palette should feel like a well-maintained house, not a hospital.

**Accent — Burnished Copper/Amber:** `#C17A47`
Warm, honest, slightly premium. Used sparingly — for the primary CTA and key trust highlights only. Communicates: quality work, value, not the cheapest but worth it. An amber/copper tone also evokes copper plumbing pipe — a subtle, intentional nod to the trade.

**Text — Near Black:** `#1A1A1A`
High contrast, easy to read on mobile. Body text on the warm white background.

**Subtle — Muted Warm Grey:** `#8C8680`
For secondary text, captions, and dividers.

### Typography

**Headings — DM Serif Display**
A confident serif with character — not stiff or formal, but authoritative. Serifs communicate established, established-terms, longevity. For a plumber who has been in business 11 years, that authority matters. Size: large, commanding on mobile.

**Body — Instrument Sans**
Clean, humanist, highly readable on small mobile screens. Not Inter (overused to the point of meaning nothing). Instrument Sans is warm but professional — it doesn't feel like a tech startup. It feels like a professional who communicates clearly.

**Size minimums:** Body text 17px on mobile (not 16px — plumbing customers are often older). Headings scale up aggressively.

### Visual Tone

**Competent. Direct. Unpretentious.**
This is a man who has been fixing pipes in Dublin 12 for 11 years. The website should feel the same way he presumably operates: shows up, does the job, charges fairly, doesn't bullshit.

**What that means in design terms:**
- No decorative elements that don't serve a function
- Layout with clear visual hierarchy — nothing cluttered
- Photography direction: real domestic settings (bathrooms, kitchens, boilers) not stock images of smiling tradespeople shaking hands
- Copy tone: plain English, direct, no marketing fluff. "Brian McGarry, serving Dublin 12 since 2014. No call-out fee for estimates. Call 087 618 2500."

**What this is NOT:**
- Not flashy or design-forward
- Not trying to look like a national chain
- Not trying to look like a tech company
- Not using words like "synergy" or "solutions"

### Imagery Direction

**Photography — Real, Domestic, Unscripted**
- Real photos of completed jobs in Dublin homes (bathroom installations, boiler replacements, pipe repairs)
- If professional photography is unavailable at launch: the site leads with typography and layout, photography added when Brian supplies it
- Absolutely no generic stock photos of smiling plumbers in hi-vis vests holding wrenches
- Before/after imagery works well for this category if available

**Icons — Minimal line icons only for service categories**
- Simple, consistent stroke weight
- Domestic in feel (a tap icon, a shower icon, a boiler icon — not abstract geometric shapes)
- Used to break up text, not replace it

## Layout & Structure

### Homepage — Priority Order

**5 sections maximum.** Everything beyond this dilutes the primary goal.

---

**Section 1: Hero — "Dublin 12 Plumber, Since 2014"**

Position: First thing seen on arrival.

Contents:
- Large headline: "Brian McGarry — Plumber, Walkinstown, Dublin 12"
- Subheadline: "Serving Dublin 12 homes for over 11 years. No job too small."
- Primary CTA: Tap-to-call button — "Call Brian: 087 618 2500" (large, always visible, above the fold on mobile)
- Secondary: Address line — "117 St. Brendan's Crescent, Walkinstown"

Why this order: The homepage hero must immediately answer two questions a new visitor has: (1) Who is this? (2) How do I contact them? For a plumber, the phone number IS the answer to both. The address confirms legitimacy and local presence.

Mobile viewport (375px): Phone number is the dominant element. Name and tagline above it. CTA button is full-width and 56px tall minimum (thumb-friendly).

---

**Section 2: Service Overview — "What Brian Does"**

Position: Immediately below the fold.

Contents: 5–6 service categories, each with a one-line description:
- Emergency Plumbing — "Burst pipes, leaks, and urgent call-outs"
- General Repairs — "Taps, toilets, sinks, and everyday plumbing issues"
- Boiler Service — "Annual servicing and breakdowns"
- Bathroom Installations — "Full and partial bathroom plumbing"
- Drain Cleaning — "Blocked sinks, showers, and outside drains"
- [Any other services Brian confirms]

Format: Icon + service name + one-line description. No long paragraphs.

Why this position: Once we've established who he is and how to call him, the next question is "does he do what I need?" Service overview answers this in under 5 seconds.

Mobile: Full-width vertical list. Icons left, text right.

---

**Section 3: Trust Architecture — "Why Trust Brian"**

Position: Third section. This is the trust layer.

Contents (in order):
- **11 Years in Business** — "Operating since 2014 — Brian has been fixing Dublin 12 homes for over a decade. That's not a marketing claim, it's a fact."
- **[RGI Registration badge — IF applicable]** — Must be established before launch. This is a significant trust signal competitors prominently display. If Brian is RGI registered, this goes here, prominently.
- **Local Address** — "117 St. Brendan's Crescent, Walkinstown, Dublin 12 — a local address, not a call centre."
- **Mobile-First Contact** — "You get Brian directly. No receptionists, no answering services."
- **No Obligation Estimates** — "Call to arrange a no-obligation quote. No hard sell."

Why this position: The visitor has now seen (1) who he is, (2) what he does. They're now asking "but why should I trust him?" This section answers that before they even think to ask. Trust signals placed here — after the service overview — mirror the natural customer evaluation process: "What do they do? → Can I trust them to do it?"

Mobile: Stacked vertically. Each trust point is a short line — not a paragraph.

---

**Section 4: Service Area — "Where Brian Works"**

Position: Fourth section.

Contents:
- Headline: "Serving Dublin 12 and surrounding areas"
- List: Walkinstown, Knocklyon, Crumlin, Drimnagh, Terenure, Perrystown, Bluebell
- Subtext: "Brian is based in Walkinstown. Most Dublin 12 jobs are within a short drive."
- Optional: A simple embedded Google Maps static image (not interactive — no need for full embed)

Why this position: Location trust. "He works in my area" is a key conversion factor for local trades. Placing it here confirms geographic relevance before the contact section.

Mobile: Simple text list. No map clutter.

---

**Section 5: Contact — "Call or Email Brian"**

Position: Last section, but with a sticky mobile CTA always visible.

Contents:
- Primary: Large tap-to-call button — "Call Brian: 087 618 2500"
- Secondary: Landline — "Or call the office: 01-4424089"
- Email: brianmcgarry90@gmail.com (no contact form — unnecessary friction for a sole trader)
- Simple text: "Best time to call: [if Brian specifies]"
- Optional: WhatsApp CTA if Brian uses it

Why no contact form: A form adds friction and creates anxiety ("will they reply?"). For a sole trader, a phone call is immediate and converts better. The goal is a phone call, not an email enquiry.

Mobile: The phone CTA dominates. Email as secondary option.

### CTA Model

**Primary CTA: Tap-to-call — 087 618 2500**

The primary action on every page, every section, and at every scroll position on mobile is a phone call.

**Sticky mobile CTA:** A fixed bar at the bottom of the mobile screen — "Tap to Call Brian — 087 618 2500" — visible at all times, does not disappear on scroll. 56px tall, full width.

**Desktop:** Phone number in top-right of header, always visible. Secondary CTA in footer.

**Secondary CTA:** Email (brianmcgarry90@gmail.com) — displayed in contact section and footer only.

**No competing CTAs.** The site does not ask the user to follow on Instagram, sign up for a newsletter, or fill in a form. One action: call Brian.

## Mobile-First Specifics

**At 375px viewport, no scroll:**
- Hero: Name + tagline visible
- Primary CTA (tap-to-call button) visible and above the fold
- Phone number is the dominant element — minimum 20px font size for the number itself

**Tap hierarchy (priority order):**
1. Call Brian (always top priority — sticky button)
2. Read services (immediate below hero)
3. Read trust signals (scroll required)
4. Email Brian (contact section)

**What collapses from desktop to mobile:**
- Header navigation (no nav menu — single page, no sub-pages needed)
- Multiple CTAs competing — only phone CTA sticky on mobile
- Map embed → simple address text
- Icon set → simplified, no decorative elements

**What stays the same:**
- Phone number is always accessible (sticky bar)
- Service list remains scannable
- Trust signals remain visible in the same order

**Performance:**
- Target: Sub-2-second load on 4G mobile
- Single-page, minimal assets
- No heavy JavaScript frameworks
- System fonts for fast render

## Differentiation Rationale

### From His Current State (Nothing)
Brian has no website, no GBP, no reviews, no content. His only digital presence is a passive 2014 directory listing with no photos and zero engagement.

The concept creates from scratch: a professional mobile-first presence that answers the three questions every new customer asks in under 10 seconds.

### From Competitor Websites (DeWAR, Dublin Area Plumbers, NRM, O'Brien)

**Competitor pattern:** All competitors present as companies — teams, multiple locations, generic service pages, large website structures with multiple CTAs and quote forms.

**Brian's differentiation:** The website makes him a person, not a company. A homeowner who is tired of calling a national chain and getting a different technician each time, filling in an online form, waiting for a callback — this website speaks directly to that frustration.

The design is intentionally NOT trying to look like DeWAR Plumbers or Dublin Area Plumbers. It is smaller, more personal, more direct. It says: I'm Brian. I live here. I've been here for 11 years. Call me.

**Specific differentiators:**
- Single-person positioning vs. multi-technician companies
- Local address (Walkinstown) vs. office/branch addresses
- Mobile-first tap-to-call vs. quote forms and online booking
- 11 years of local history vs. company age claims
- Direct accountability (he answers his phone) vs. call centre routing

### From Generic Template Websites

Generic template: Hero image → 3-column service icons → generic testimonial carousel → contact form → footer.

Brian's concept: Name + phone number as hero → confirmed services → trust signals built around his specific story → simple contact. No carousel. No generic stock photo. No form asking for their name, email, phone, message, and preferred appointment time.

## GBP Integration

The website and GBP are not separate workstreams. They are one local search strategy.

**How they work together:**

1. **GBP Claim/Setup → Brian's website → Local SEO boost**
   - Brian claims his Google Business Profile (or Forge assists) using the address: 117 St. Brendan's Crescent, Walkinstown, Dublin 12
   - Website URL is added to GBP listing
   - GBP category: "Plumber" + "Emergency Plumbing Service"
   - NAP (Name, Address, Phone) on website exactly matches GBP — consistent signals to Google's local algorithm

2. **GBP Reviews → Website trust signals**
   - As Brian accumulates GBP reviews (0 currently — this will take time), website can display: "Rated on Google" with average score
   - Until reviews accumulate: website does not fake a rating — it uses 11 years in business and direct testimonials instead

3. **GBP Posts → Local engagement**
   - GBP allows short posts (offers, seasonal tips, new service areas)
   - Low effort, high local SEO value — can be done in under 5 minutes per month

4. **GBP Questions → FAQ content**
   - GBP Q&A is public — common questions (e.g., "Do you do emergency callouts?", "Do you charge for estimates?") can be pre-seeded on GBP and answered on the website FAQ

**GBP is not optional for this lead.** It is the single highest-impact, zero-cost action Brian can take. The website concept should explicitly position GBP setup as a paired deliverable: "Your website will work with your Google Business Profile to make you visible in Dublin 12 local search."
