# Skill: Orion Evidence Discipline

## When to use this skill

When starting any research task, verification request, or evidence-gathering brief.

---

## Core Discipline Pattern

**Collect evidence. Label certainty. Synthesize carefully.**

This is the irreducible Orion research workflow.

---

## Step 1 — Collect Evidence

Find sources. Verify claims directly — don't rely on summaries.

- Fetch primary sources, not just search snippets
- Assess source reliability: High / Medium / Low
- Cite with: source name, URL, date accessed, reliability rating
- Track contradictions — they are signal, not noise

---

## Step 2 — Label Certainty

Every finding must be tagged:

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Confirmed** | Source available, directly verified |
| **Inferred** | Likely based on evidence, but not directly confirmed |
| **Unknown** | Insufficient data to say with confidence |

Do not present inferred findings as confirmed. Do not hide uncertainty.

---

## Step 3 — Synthesize Carefully

Bring findings together into a coherent picture.

- Lead with what's confirmed
- Distinguish between facts and interpretation
- Flag contradictions explicitly
- State implications — what does this mean for the original question?
- Be specific: prices, dates, names, limits, not vague summaries

---

## Quality Standards

A good Orion deliverable:
- Names sources and dates
- Separates known from likely from unknown
- Flags contradictions rather than smoothing them over
- States implications for the original brief
- Is concise when the evidence allows, thorough when the question warrants

---

## Frustrations to Avoid

- Treating a single source as confirmation
- Treating inference as fact
- Long summaries that say the same thing three ways
- Bulk without discrimination — more sources is not better evidence

---

## Escalation Triggers

Escalate to Nero when:
- Research surfaces something requiring a business decision
- Findings have implications beyond the research scope
- The research question itself was wrong or incomplete
- Something is time-sensitive
