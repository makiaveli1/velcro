# COMPETITOR_SCAN.md — Competitor Scan Runbook

_When analyzing competitors, do this._

---

## Step 1 — Define the Scope

Who are the competitors?
- Direct competitors (same market, same audience)
- Indirect competitors (same problem, different approach)
- Adjacent players (overlapping audience)

What questions are we trying to answer?
- Pricing?
- Positioning?
- Product features?
- Go-to-market strategy?

---

## Step 2 — Information Sources

- Company websites and pricing pages
- G2, Capterra, Trustpilot reviews
- LinkedIn company pages
- Crunchbase / PitchBook for funding
- Product Hunt launches
- Industry reports
- Reddit, Hacker News, Twitter/X for candid takes

---

## Step 3 — Per Competitor

For each competitor, collect:

**Basics**
- Company name and URL
- What they do (one sentence)
- Target market / audience
- Founding year and funding stage

**Offer**
- Core product(s) and pricing
- Key differentiators
- What's included vs. extra

**Positioning**
- Headline and tagline
- Key messages
- Proof points (social proof, logos)

**Strengths** (what they do well)
- Confirmed from reviews or direct evidence

**Weaknesses** (what customers complain about)
- Confirmed from negative reviews or complaints

**Market presence**
- Estimated market share or reach
- Geographic focus
- Sales motion (PLG, SLG, both)

---

## Step 4 — Comparative Matrix

Build a comparison:

| Dimension | Us | Competitor A | Competitor B |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pricing | ? | ? | ? |

| Key feature 2 | ✓/✗ | ✓/✗ | ✓/✗ |
| Target market | ? | ? | ? |
| Strength | ? | ? | ? |
| Weakness | ? | ? | ? |

---

## Step 5 — Deliverable Format

```
## Competitor Scan: [market/segment]

### Competitors Overview
[brief summary of landscape]

### Competitor A — [name]
[structured findings per above]

### Competitor B — [name]
...

### Competitive Matrix
[comparison table]

### Strategic Implications
- [opportunity for us based on gaps]
- [threat to watch]
- [what to avoid — already well-served]

### Confidence
HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW — [reason]
```

---

## Quality Rules

- Cite specific sources for specific claims
- Negative findings must come from reviews or direct evidence — not speculation
- "Seems to be" is not a confirmed fact — say "appears to be based on X"
- If pricing is not publicly available, note "not publicly disclosed"
