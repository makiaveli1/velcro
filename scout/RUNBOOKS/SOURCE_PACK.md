# SOURCE_PACK.md — Source Pack Runbook

_When asked to compile a source pack, do this._

---

## What Is a Source Pack?

A curated collection of sources on a specific topic — with summaries, reliability ratings, and relevance assessments. Ready for further research or decision-making.

---

## Step 1 — Define the Topic

What is the topic? What questions should these sources answer?

Ask if unclear before starting.

---

## Step 2 — Source Discovery

Use multiple search approaches:
- Web search on primary and related terms
- Academic/paper databases if relevant
- Industry publications
- Direct company sources (annual reports, filings, blog posts)
- Community sources (Reddit, HN, forums)

---

## Step 3 — Per Source Assessment

For each source:

**Source name and type** (article, report, paper, blog, review, video)
**URL**
**Date published**
**Date accessed**
**Author/organization** (and their credibility)
**Core content** — what does it say in 1-3 sentences?
**Key claims** — specific data points, assertions
**Reliability** — HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW (and why)
**Relevance** — HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW (to the topic)
**What it confirms vs. what it suggests**

---

## Step 4 — Organize

Group by theme or claim type. Flag contradictions between sources.

---

## Step 5 — Source Pack Format

```
## Source Pack: [topic]

### Overview
[what this pack covers, how many sources, date range]

### Source 1: [title]
- **Type:** [article/report/paper/etc.]
- **URL:** [link]
- **Published:** [date]
- **Accessed:** [date]
- **Author:** [name/org]
- **Reliability:** HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW
- **Relevance:** HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW
- **Summary:** [1-3 sentences]
- **Key claims:** [bullets]
- **Notes:** [anything notable]

### Source 2: ...

### Cross-Cutting Themes
[patterns across sources]

### Contradictions
[sources that disagree and on what]

### Confidence
HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW — [reason]
```

---

## Quality Rules

- Include only sources I actually read and can summarize
- Never include a source without assessing its reliability
- Note if a source has a conflict of interest (e.g., a vendor writing about their own product)
- If I cannot access a source, note "could not verify"
