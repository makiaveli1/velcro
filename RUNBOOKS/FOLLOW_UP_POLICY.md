# Runbook — Follow-Up Policy

_How we follow up without becoming the thing we hate._

---

## Purpose

Follow-up is where good outreach systems earn trust or lose it. A disciplined, respectful follow-up sequence creates genuine opportunities. A pushy, spammy follow-up sequence destroys reputation and gets marked as junk.

The rules here are strict by design. We would rather lose a lead than become an irritant.

---

## The Follow-Up Sequence

### Touch 1 — Initial Outreach (Day 0)
- Sent after Nero approval + send timing confirmation
- Full pitch (Mercury draft + Nero approved)
- Logged in STATUS.md with exact timestamp
- Logged in campaign results

### Touch 2 — Follow-Up #1 (Day 4)
- If no response to Touch 1
- New angle or perspective — not the same email resent
- Shorter than Touch 1
- Approved by Nero before sending
- Logged in STATUS.md

### Touch 3 — Follow-Up #2 (Day 10)
- If no response to Touch 2
- Different angle again — new observation or market context
- Softer tone than Touch 2
- Approved by Nero before sending
- Logged in STATUS.md

### Touch 4 — Final (Day 21)
- If no response to Touch 3
- Graceful close — acknowledge the silence, leave door open
- Ultra-low pressure: "If this is ever relevant in future"
- Approved by Nero before sending
- Logged in STATUS.md

### After Touch 4 — NO_RESPONSE
- Move to NO_RESPONSE status
- Add to suppression list (6-month cooldown minimum)
- No further contact unless significant new context

---

## What Constitutes a "Response"

| Response Type | Action |
|---|---|
| Explicit "not interested" | Suppress immediately. No further contact. Log graciously. |
| "Remove me / unsubscribe" | Suppress immediately. Comply immediately. No further contact. |
| "I'm not looking for this right now" | Acknowledge. Note in STATUS.md. Park. May revisit in 3–6 months with new context. |
| "Who are you / how did you get my details?" | Do NOT double down. Explain briefly and offer to remove if preferred. Suppress if requested. |
| "We're already working with someone" | Acknowledge gracefully. Note in STATUS.md. Do not disparage the other provider. |
| Silence | Continue the follow-up sequence. Do not interpret silence as refusal. |
| Any positive response | Route to Nero immediately. This is now a warm lead. |

## Follow-Up Content Rules

### Rule 1 — Each touch must add value
"bumping this" or "just following up" is not acceptable unless there's genuinely new information to share.

**What counts as new value:**
- A related market observation we didn't mention in the previous touch
- A different angle on the opportunity (not just rephrasing the same point)
- A brief example of a similar business we helped (without breaching confidentiality)
- A question that reframes the situation
- If you have nothing new to add — don't send this follow-up. Park the lead.

### Rule 2 — Never be a broken record
If you're sending the same email with different words, you're doing it wrong. The sequence should feel like a thoughtful conversation that progresses, not an automated reminder sequence.

### Rule 3 — Never guilt, pressure, or passive-aggressive framing
- ❌ "Just bumping this up in your inbox"
- ❌ "Not sure if you saw my last email"
- ❌ "I know you're busy"
- ❌ "Did you get a chance to read my previous message?"
- ❌ "Following up as promised"
- ✅ "Hi [Name] — wanted to share a different angle on what we found..."
- ✅ "Hi [Name] — a quick observation from looking at [their market]..."

### Rule 4 — Timing rules
- Do not send follow-ups on weekends
- Do not send follow-ups before 9am or after 6pm local time
- Avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons
- Respect local public holidays

### Rule 5 — Channel discipline
- If initial outreach was via email, all follow-ups via email
- If initial outreach was via LinkedIn, follow-ups via LinkedIn
- Do not escalate to phone without explicit Nero instruction
- Do not add extra channels (e.g., email + LinkedIn + cold call) without explicit Nero instruction

## Managing the Follow-Up Queue

Mercury maintains a follow-up tracking system in the lead STATUS.md:

```markdown
## Follow-Up Log
| Touch | Date | Channel | Content | Content Approved | Deployment Approved | Sent |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | YYYY-MM-DD | Email | Full pitch | Yes (Nero) | Yes (Nero) | Sent |
| 2 | YYYY-MM-DD | Email | New angle on market gap | Yes (Nero) | Yes (Nero) | Sent |
| 3 | Pending | — | — | — | — | — |
```

Before any follow-up is sent, Mercury checks:
1. Is this the correct touch number?
2. Is the correct number of days elapsed since the last touch?
3. Is there a new angle/value to add?
4. Does Nero approve this specific follow-up content?
5. **Has Nero separately approved deployment of this specific follow-up touch?** (Content approval and deployment approval are separate gates)

## When to Stop Early

Stop the sequence and suppress even if it's only Touch 1 or Touch 2 if:
- The business asks to be removed
- The business explicitly declines
- The business indicates they are already being pitched heavily
- There's any sign that continued contact would damage reputation
- Nero instructs to stop

**Never continue a follow-up sequence that is creating negative signal. Better to lose the lead than create a detractor.**

---

## Re-Engagement Rules

A suppressed or no-response lead may be re-engaged only if:

1. **Minimum 6 months have passed** since last contact
2. **Significant new context exists** — e.g., the business launched a new website (and it's weak), they expanded locations, they have new Google reviews that show growth, a new competitor entered their market
3. **Nero approves the re-engagement** with specific new context cited

Re-engagement is a new outreach sequence — it does not inherit goodwill from the previous one. The business may not remember you. Treat it as a new lead entirely.

---

## What We Track

After each follow-up sequence (won, lost, or no-response), log:
- Total touches sent
- Time from first touch to final disposition
- Response rate (if any)
- Any response patterns worth noting

This data helps us understand what follow-up content works and when we should stop.
